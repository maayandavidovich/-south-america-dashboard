💡 Key Tips:
Route direction: Now flows in mostly one direction – south to Ushuaia, then steadily north all the way to Salta. The Pucón→El Bolsón→Bariloche zigzag is gone (El Bolsón is now a day trip from Bariloche instead of a separate stop). One small unavoidable dip remains: El Chaltén → Puerto Natales briefly goes south again, because Torres del Paine's dates are fixed and El Chaltén sits north of it – fixing that would mean inventing several extra filler days elsewhere.
Carretera Austral: 8 nights – always carry extra fuel, confirm rental car is Chile-approved and can be dropped in Argentina (Bariloche).
Cerro Castillo: Full 3–4 day trek – needs a CONAF permit booked in advance, daily quota is limited.
Villarrica volcano: Physically demanding full-day climb, weather cancels it often in shoulder season – build in flexibility if possible.
El Bolsón day trip: ~4h round-trip from Bariloche – enough for the market and a short walk, not much more.
W Trek Day 1: Start at 6:30am for Las Torres sunrise. Day 3 (Paine Grande→Grey) is 24km – leave early!
Torres del Paine check-in: Show passport + PDI migration card for VAT exemption. Check-in 14:30–21:00 only. Dates are fixed – don't move these.
Sleeping bag: Rent -5°C rated bag in Puerto Natales (Erratic Rock). Only needed for Francés campsite night.
Iguazu → Salta: No direct flights – almost certainly connects through Buenos Aires. Check timing carefully.
Salta: 2 nights – prioritize one full-day Purmamarca + Salinas Grandes excursion and keep the second day for Salta city. Skip Iruya unless you extend the stay.
2–4 Dec – relaxed finish: Salta → Buenos Aires on 2 Dec, then two nights in the city. On 4 Dec travel directly to EZE for the 23:00 flight, with no domestic-flight connection risk.
